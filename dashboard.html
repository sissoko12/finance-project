Liquidity runway and synthetic credit for the S&P 500, from the latest annual (10-K) filing per company. Burn mode (burn) measures months of liquid assets against a company's actual negative operating cash flow. Stress overlay (stress) applies to cash-generative firms: a severe revenue-to-zero stress — months of liquid assets against full operating opex (COGS + OpEx + SG&A). These are different metrics; a “Critical” stress flag means thin cash vs. total opex, not imminent default. Synthetic rating maps EBIT ÷ interest coverage to a Damodaran default spread (financial vs. non-financial breakpoint tables). Click any row for the full calculation breakdown.
